# Investment Policy Model (IPM)

## Purpose

The Investment Policy Model (IPM) defines the **decision boundaries**
within which APE operates.

It is the **single source of truth** for:
- portfolio intent,
- acceptable risk,
- permitted actions,
- rebalancing philosophy.

If a recommendation conflicts with the IPM, **the recommendation is invalid**.

---

## What an IPM Is (and Is Not)

### The IPM Is
- a constitutional document governing decisions,
- stable across market regimes,
- explicit about constraints and priorities,
- designed to prevent behavioural and process errors.

### The IPM Is Not
- a market outlook,
- a performance forecast,
- a tactical trading plan,
- a document that changes frequently.

---

## Core Components of an IPM

### 1. Investment Objectives

An IPM must state:
- the primary long-term objective (e.g. real wealth growth),
- secondary objectives (liquidity, stability, simplicity),
- the trade-offs the investor is willing to accept.

---

### 2. Strategic Asset Allocation (SAA)

The IPM must define:
- the permitted asset classes,
- long-term target weights,
- the intent of each asset class (growth, defence, liquidity).

Target weights express **long-term intent**, not short-term views.

---

### 3. Risk Framework

The IPM must distinguish clearly between:

- **Risk capacity**  
  (objective ability to bear loss; a hard constraint)

- **Risk tolerance**  
  (behavioural comfort; a soft constraint)

Risk capacity always overrides tolerance.

---

### 4. Rebalancing Philosophy

The IPM must specify:
- how drift is measured,
- what constitutes “material” deviation,
- when rebalancing is required,
- when inaction is preferred.

Rebalancing exists to **control risk**, not to maximise return.

---

### 5. Constraints

The IPM must explicitly state:
- prohibited actions (e.g. leverage, market timing),
- liquidity requirements,
- wrapper or tax considerations (if applicable).

Unstated constraints are treated as unknown risk.

---

## Authority and Change Control

The IPM:
- cannot be modified implicitly,
- cannot evolve silently,
- must be versioned when changed.

Any change requires:
1. an updated IPM version, and
2. a documented rationale.

---

## Relationship to the System

The IPM governs and constrains:
- deterministic portfolio evaluation,
- recommendation eligibility,
- explanation requirements,
- future automation.

APE defers to the IPM at all times.

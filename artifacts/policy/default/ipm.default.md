# Investment Policy Model (IPM)

**Project:** **AI Portfolio Decision Co-Pilot (Automated Portfolio Evaluator)**  
**Owner:** Default  
**Date:** 27 January 2026  
**Version:** 0.1-default  
**Scope:** Self-directed, long-term investment decision support  
**Jurisdictional context:** UK  

---

## 1. Purpose of the Investment Policy Model

The Investment Policy Model defines the decision boundaries within which the AI Portfolio Decision Co-Pilot (Automated Portfolio Evaluator) operates.

If a recommendation conflicts with this document, the recommendation is invalid.

---

## 2. Investment Objectives

### Primary Objective

Grow long-term real wealth steadily while avoiding avoidable behavioural and process-driven errors.

### Secondary Objectives

- Maintain sufficient liquidity to avoid forced asset sales.
- Control drawdowns relative to risk capacity.
- Minimise unnecessary costs and portfolio turnover.
- Keep decisions explainable and reviewable.

---

## 3. Investment Philosophy

- Long-term outcomes are driven primarily by asset allocation.
- Broad diversification is preferred over concentration.
- Costs, taxes, and turnover are controllable.
- Rebalancing manages risk rather than predicting returns.
- Complexity must justify itself.

The default posture is conservative with respect to change.

---

## 4. Strategic Asset Allocation (SAA)

### Policy Portfolio Structure

Multi-asset, globally diversified, index-oriented.

### Core Asset Classes

- Global Equities
- High-Quality Bonds
- Cash / Money Market Instruments

### Target Weights (Default Fail-Safe)

- Equities: 60%
- Bonds: 35%
- Cash: 5%

---

## 5. Risk Reality and Constraints

### Risk Capacity

Risk capacity cannot be overridden by risk tolerance.

### Drawdown Guardrail

Maximum acceptable rolling 12-month drawdown: 20%

If approached or exceeded, default response is defer and review.

---

## 6. Rebalancing Philosophy

### Trigger Framework

Band-based rebalancing:

- Equities: ±7% absolute
- Bonds: ±6% absolute
- Cash: ±3% absolute

### Turnover Control

Minimum trade threshold:
- £500 or 0.5% of portfolio value (whichever is higher)

---

## 7. Liquidity Management

Maintain a dedicated cash or near-cash sleeve. Liquidity needs override return optimisation.

---

## 8. Costs, Taxes, and Wrappers

Minimise avoidable costs and respect wrapper constraints.

---

## 9. Permitted and Prohibited Actions

### Prohibited by Default

- Leverage and margin
- Concentrated exposure
- Market timing by sentiment
- Silent policy changes

---

## 10. Decision Authority and Change Control

Policy parameters cannot be changed silently. Changes require versioning and documentation.

---

## 11. Review Cadence

Monthly check, quarterly review, ad hoc only for genuine life events.

---

### Status

This document represents a **safe default policy** used when no local policy is present.

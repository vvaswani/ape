## 5. Risk Reality and Constraints

### Risk Capacity (Hard Constraint)

Risk capacity is determined by:

- liquidity needs,
- income stability,
- external concentrated risks, and
- time horizon.

Risk capacity sets an upper bound on acceptable portfolio risk and **cannot be overridden by risk tolerance**.

### Risk Tolerance (Soft Constraint)

Risk tolerance reflects behavioural comfort and influences:

- communication style,
- review cadence,
- acceptable volatility bands.

It does not permit exceeding risk capacity.

### Drawdown Guardrail

Maximum acceptable rolling 12-month drawdown: ___%

If this level is approached or exceeded, the default response is **defer and review**.

## 6. Rebalancing Philosophy

Rebalancing exists to maintain intended risk exposure over time.

### Trigger Framework

The preferred approach is **band-based rebalancing**.

- Rebalance when an asset class deviates by ±___% (absolute) from its target weight, or
- when aggregate equity exposure deviates materially from policy intent.

Calendar-based reviews are secondary and exist only to check for drift.

### Rebalancing Hierarchy

1. Use new contributions to correct underweights.
2. If selling is required, reduce overweights to fund underweights.
3. Avoid trades that create cosmetic alignment at the cost of unnecessary turnover.

### Turnover Control

- Minimum trade threshold: £___ or ___% of portfolio.
- Small deviations may be tolerated to reduce friction.

## 7. Liquidity Management

- A dedicated cash or near-cash sleeve is maintained.
- Liquidity must cover foreseeable spending and commitments without reliance on asset sales.
- Liquidity decisions take precedence over return optimisation.

---

## 8. Costs, Taxes, and Wrappers

- The system must minimise avoidable fees, spreads, and transaction costs.
- Account wrappers (ISA, SIPP, GIA, trust structures) must be respected.
- Tax efficiency is considered where relevant, but must not prevent necessary risk management actions.

---

## 9. Permitted and Prohibited Actions

### Permitted

- Maintaining Strategic Asset Allocation via contributions and disciplined rebalancing.
- Minor tactical tilts only if explicitly defined, capped, and documented.

### Prohibited by Default

- Leverage, margin, or borrowing against portfolio assets.
- Concentrated single-asset or single-security exposure.
- Market timing based on sentiment or short-term forecasts.
- Strategy changes without an updated Portfolio Guidelines version.

---

## 10. Decision Authority and Change Control

- The AI Portfolio Decision Co-Pilot cannot modify policy parameters silently.
- Any change to targets, bands, or permitted assets requires:
  1. an updated Portfolio Guidelines version, and
  2. a recorded rationale captured in a Decision Snapshot.

---

## 11. Review Cadence

- **Monthly:** Lightweight check for drift, contributions, and constraint changes.
- **Quarterly:** Formal review against Strategic Asset Allocation and policy intent.
- **Ad hoc:** Only for genuine life events or material constraint changes.

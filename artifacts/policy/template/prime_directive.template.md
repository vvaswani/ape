
# Prime Directive Statement

**Project:** **AI Portfolio Decision Co-Pilot (Automated Portfolio Evaluator)**
**Owner:** Harish
**Date:** 27th January 2026
**Version:** 0.1

---

## Prime Directive

The **AI Portfolio Decision Co-Pilot (Automated Portfolio Evaluator)** exists to help a long-term investor make **sound, repeatable portfolio decisions** with **clarity, discipline, and a durable audit trail**.

---

## What the AI Portfolio Decision Co-Pilot Must Protect (Non-Negotiable)

### The Plan Beats the Mood

The system must actively prevent impulse-driven changes caused by headlines, fear, short-term regret, or market noise.

### Risk Comes Before Return

The system must never recommend actions that violate the investor’s risk capacity, liquidity requirements, or known constraints.

### Explainability Is Mandatory

Every recommendation must be accompanied by:

* a clear rationale,
* stated assumptions, and
* explicit disclosure of uncertainty.

### Human Remains Accountable

The system advises; the human decides.
Recommending **“do nothing”** must always be treated as a valid and often appropriate outcome.

---

## What Success Looks Like

For any decision, the user can clearly answer:

> **What changed? What did we do? Why?**

Success also means:

* Recommendations are consistently aligned with the **Investment Policy Model (IPM)**.
* Inaction is recommended when action is not justified.
* The system produces **Decision Snapshots** that can be reviewed, compared, and audited over time.

---

## What the AI Portfolio Decision Co-Pilot Explicitly Will Not Do

* **No trade execution:** It does not place orders or move money.
* **No financial advice claims:** It provides decision support for a self-directed investor.
* **No market timing or prediction:** It does not forecast markets or claim informational edge.
* **No optimisation theatre:** It does not pursue “optimal” allocations based on fragile or opaque assumptions.
* **No silent strategy drift:** It will not change the policy portfolio without explicit user approval and documented rationale.

---

## Default Safety Rule

> If required inputs are missing, ambiguous, or confidence is low, the system must **pause**, ask targeted clarifying questions, or recommend **“defer and review.”**
